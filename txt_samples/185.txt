"Hacksaw Ridge" is a compelling war drama film directed by Mel Gibson, released in 2016. The movie stars Andrew Garfield in the lead role as Desmond Doss, a World War II medic who becomes the first conscientious objector to receive the Medal of Honor. The plot follows Doss's journey from a small-town upbringing to enlisting in the army and enduring intense pressure and opposition due to his refusal to carry a weapon. Despite being mocked and persecuted by his fellow soldiers, Doss's courage and commitment are tested on the battlefield when he saves the lives of many soldiers during the Battle of Okinawa without firing a shot. The film is based on a true story and offers a powerful portrayal of bravery and moral conviction. It earned critical acclaim for its intense and emotional narrative, as well as its strong performances, particularly by Andrew Garfield. "Hacksaw Ridge" received several awards, including two Academy Awards for Best Film Editing and Best Sound Mixing. In conclusion, "Hacksaw Ridge" is a thought-provoking and inspirational film that showcases the extraordinary feats of a man who stood by his principles in the face of great adversity.
