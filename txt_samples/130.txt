In the development of public transportation in the United States, there are several major trends. First, there is an increasing focus on sustainable and environmentally friendly transportation, such as the development of electricity-based transportation and the use of renewable energy. Second, there is a shift towards more connected and integrated transport systems, allowing people to switch seamlessly between different modes of transport, including trains, buses and bicycles. Third, information technology and mobile applications have changed the way people plan and use public transportation, with increasingly widespread adoption of ride-sharing services, such as Uber and Lyft, as well as apps that enable payment and real-time monitoring of rides. Overall, there are ongoing efforts to improve accessibility, efficiency, and traffic in the public transportation system in the United States
